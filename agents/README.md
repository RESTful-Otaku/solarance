## What is this?

Solarance: Beginnings is a **cozy, persistent, co-op space sandbox** — the game for the player EVE Online traumatized and lost. No required combat. No gankers. No ticking clock when you log off.

The core loop is simple: **find → extract → haul → contribute → watch it grow.** You fly out to an asteroid sector, mine some ore, haul it back to a partially-built station, and add your contribution to the pile. Other players are doing the same. Over weeks and months, you watch your shared station grow into something none of you could have built alone.

It is built for the player who has 20 minutes between household demands — sometimes a Saturday afternoon, sometimes not at all that week. Sessions are short. Progress persists. Returning never means falling behind.

**Reference points:** Melvor Idle (idle-friendly retention), No Man's Sky peaceful mode (a world that doesn't threaten), Old School RuneScape (respects intermittent play), EVE Online cooperative sov-building (minus the PvP).

## Current status

Early development — pre-MVP. One developer, working evenings and weekends.

The current focus is the **small-scale shared-building spike**: can two or more players haul resources to a partially-built station and feel that climactic *"we built this together"* moment? Everything else — mining minigames, NPCs, factions with real lore, combat — waits until that core emotional beat is proven to land.

If you want the full scope and the rules-of-engagement for what does and doesn't make the cut, the [MVP design doc](https://github.com/galaxycr8r/solarance-beginnings/blob/main/docs/Solarance_Beginnings_MVP_Design_Doc.md) is the single source of truth.
